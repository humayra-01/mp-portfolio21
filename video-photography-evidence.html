Photography Basics

Landscape is one of my favourite types of photography because they show great depth, moreover they also showcase ones surroundings in a different light enabling the viewer to visit the place. “Light is one of the most important factors in any photograph, but even more so in landscape photography” (Dadfar n.d.). For my photos I adjusted the RGB levels adding to the red and blue slightly decreasing the green. I made photos more vibrant and a higher contrast and went for low lighting shots so I could decrease the brightness. For one image I added layers of the same image but adjusted these layers using the gradient map and then decreased the opacity to give it just a touch of different colours and moods. In my silhouette images I decreased the shadows and faded the images to near black to give them a silhouette feature making the background the centre of focus. All my photos are landscape with the same almost mirroring colour theme running through them. I also played around with the levels on my images making some parts of the images darker and some lighter.

Video production Basics

Video production basics: For recording our video we didn’t use a tripod but just a video camera and our composition was a hand held camera so it seemed to be informal and comedic. For the editing we changed the colours to adjust the brightness using curves in a result to try get the noise in the picture down as at first it was grainy and not very clear.
