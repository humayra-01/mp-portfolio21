Typography

The difference between serif and sans serif is that “a serif is a decorative stroke that finishes off the end of a letters stem (sometimes also called the “feet” of the letters). In turn, a serif font is a font that has serifs, while a sans serif is a font that does not (hence the “sans”).” (Rinaldi.2019) I have used a sans serif font that contrasts the utopian theme that runs through my images as well as this the colour of the font matches the theme of imagery. The contrast between the font and the imagery implies that the font is used to convey simplicity and even minimalism so it is recognised but isn’t the focus of attention and the imagery is. The font gives a sense of modernity and maturity whilst the implications of a utopian theme implies a perfect world one like children would think of as they haven’t matured yet. I have chosen a formal font to contrast informal imagery to keep both in focus but to make the imagery more important. The sans serif font I have used is used in the header and in the texts as I didn’t want to overcrowd it with too many fonts and make them the focus.
